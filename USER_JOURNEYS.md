# User Journeys: The Arc of Connection

This document outlines the user's path through the Personal Relationship OS over their first year. The journey is designed to be gradual, calming, and continually compounding in value, avoiding the immediate overwhelming data-entry demands of traditional CRMs.

## 1. Day 1 (The Sanctuary)
**User Goals:**
- Understand the core philosophy of the product.
- Experience immediate relief from "connection anxiety."
- Create a safe, private space for their most important relationships.

**User Emotions:**
- *Entering:* Overwhelmed by external noise, guilty about neglected connections, curious.
- *Leaving:* Calmer, grounded, feeling a sense of intentionality and a sigh of relief.

**User Actions:**
- Read the core philosophy and anti-goals.
- Add 3 to 5 people to their `Core` circle.
- Write one deep reflection or capture a meaningful memory about one of those core people.

**Friction Points:**
- "Blank page" anxiety when asked to reflect.
- The urge to mass-import contacts from their phone (which we intentionally restrict/discourage to maintain quality).

**Success Criteria:**
- User successfully anchors at least 3 `Core` relationships.
- User authors at least one piece of meaningful reflection, experiencing the aesthetic calmness of the editor.

## 2. Week 1 (The Habituation)
**User Goals:**
- Integrate the OS into their weekly rhythm.
- Begin capturing serendipitous thoughts or hooks naturally.
- Feel a lower cognitive load regarding social obligations.

**User Emotions:**
- Reflective, attentive, and gently prompted (not harassed or notified).

**User Actions:**
- Log an `Interaction` (e.g., a phone call or coffee date) with a few notes.
- Park an `Aspirational Goal` or `Shared Interest` hook for someone.
- Expand their circles slightly to include a few `Orbit` connections.

**Friction Points:**
- Remembering to use the app after an interaction, since we do not send aggressive push notifications.
- Feeling like they must write a novel for every interaction (mitigated by offering light `Check-in` action types).

**Success Criteria:**
- User logs an interaction that occurred *after* Day 1.
- User visits the app unprompted at least once during the week.

## 3. Month 1 (The Insight)
**User Goals:**
- See the value of compounded context.
- Prepare for an upcoming interaction using historical notes.
- Move beyond initial setup into sustainable maintenance.

**User Emotions:**
- Confident, prepared, and deeply appreciative of their own past documentation.

**User Actions:**
- Review a person's profile before a planned `Shared Experience`.
- Notice an `Initiated`/`Received` imbalance and decide to be the one to reach out to a friend.
- Transition a relationship stage (e.g., acknowledging an acquaintance is entering `Cultivation` or a close friend is in a season of `Drift`).

**Friction Points:**
- Guilt over falling behind if they missed a few weeks (the UI must reassure them that gaps are natural and fine).
- Managing connections that drift naturally without pathologizing the silence.

**Success Criteria:**
- User accesses historical context prior to a new interaction.
- User has logged activities across multiple domains (`Personal`, `Professional`, `Community`).

## 4. Month 3 (The Deepening)
**User Goals:**
- Trust the OS as the definitive external brain for relationship context.
- Start bridging connections or using hooks to provide meaningful value.
- Reflect on the macro-seasons of their social life.

**User Emotions:**
- Grounded, generous, and highly present in conversations.

**User Actions:**
- Act on a stored `Resource/Recommendation` hook (e.g., sharing a book they noted a friend would love).
- Experience a relationship naturally moving from `Cultivation` to `Maturation`.
- Perform a quarterly reflection on their own energetic capacity and relationship balance.

**Friction Points:**
- The list of `Orbit` connections might become cluttered if not periodically reviewed or allowed to naturally fade into `Dormant` states.

**Success Criteria:**
- User proactively initiates an interaction based on a previously stored hook.
- Evidence of long-form, vulnerable reflections indicating high trust in the platform's privacy.

## 5. Year 1 (The Harvest)
**User Goals:**
- Review the overarching narrative of a year spent intentionally.
- See the tangible deepening of `Core` relationships.
- Feel a profound sense of relational wealth that transcends digital metrics.

**User Emotions:**
- Gratitude, nostalgia, deep satisfaction, and emotional security.

**User Actions:**
- Read through highly detailed, chronological histories of their closest bonds.
- Renew a `Dormant` relationship that rested for the winter.
- Set intentions for the relational energy they want to cultivate in Year 2.

**Friction Points:**
- Navigating grief or changes if relationships ended or fundamentally altered during the year. The OS must handle these transitions with grace, not transactional deletion.

**Success Criteria:**
- Sustained user retention at month 12.
- User can point to a relationship that was saved, deepened, or fundamentally improved because they had the space to be intentional.
